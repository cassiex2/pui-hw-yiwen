It's rewind time

1940's

Daisy Duck made her debut with a more sophisticated and feminine appearance than other female duck characters at the time. She had long eyelashes, a bow on her head, and a short ruffled skirt. Her personality was more assertive compared to earlier female characters in Disney cartoons.

1950's

In this era, Daisy's design didn't change much from her original appearance. She retained her feminine qualities and began to appear more regularly in Disney comics, where her appearance was standardized. Her bow became a key feature, often complemented by a matching blouse and heeled shoes.

1970's

During these decades, Daisy's appearance was refined and streamlined for various comic appearances. Her design became more consistent, with more attention given to her clothing, often reflecting the fashion of the times.

1990's

This decade saw Daisy in various roles in different TV shows and movies. For instance, in "Quack Pack," she was portrayed as a modern, career-oriented woman. Her clothing was updated to reflect a more contemporary style compared to her earlier appearances.

2000's

In the 2000s, Daisy continued to be featured in various media, including the TV show "House of Mouse." Her appearance was modernized slightly but still maintained her classic features like the bow and heeled shoes. The use of computer animation in some productions gave her a more three-dimensional look.
